</span><span class="bold italic">, Accepted 5th July 2022</span></div><p class="bold italic">First published on 3rd August 2022</p><hr><div class="abstract"><h2>Abstract</h2><p>Due to the low price and abundant reserves of sodium resources, sodium-ion batteries have become the main candidate for the next generation of energy storage equipment, particularly for large-scale grid storage and low-speed electric vehicles. Transition metal selenides have attracted considerable attention because of their high reversible capacity, superior electrical conductivity and versatile structures. In this study, two-dimensional CuSe nanosheets are synthesized <span class="italic">via</span> a simple hydrothermal reaction. When acting as an electrode material for sodium-ion batteries, the CuSe electrode exhibits an initial coulombic efficiency of 96.7% at a current density of 0.1 A g<small><sup>1</sup></small> and a specific capacity of 330 mA h g<small><sup>1</sup></small> after 100 operation cycles, as well as retains a specific capacity of 211 mA h g<small><sup>1</sup></small> even at a high current density of 10 A g<small><sup>1</sup></small>. Moreover, the anode delivers a specific capacity of 236 mA h g<small><sup>1</sup></small> after 3300 cycles at 5 A g<small><sup>1</sup></small> with a capacity retention of 91.2%. In sodium-ion hybrid capacitors (SHICs) with the two-dimensional CuSe nanosheets and Ti<small><sub>3</sub></small>C<small><sub>2</sub></small>T<small><sub><span class="italic">x</span></sub></small> MXene as the negative and positive materials, respectively, the nanosheets without any pre-sodiation present a lifespan of up to 2000 cycles at 2 A g<small><sup>1</sup></small> and a capacity retention of about 77.7%.</p></div><hr>
<h2 id="sect295"><span class="a_heading">1. Introduction</span></h2>
<span>With the technological advancement of lithium-ion batteries (LIBs), they have been applied in various fields of life.<a title="Select to navigate to reference" href="#cit1"><sup><span class="sup_ref">14</span></sup></a> However, LIBs have been greatly limited in large-scale market applications due to the scarcity and high cost of lithium resources. Hence, it is urgent to develop other alternative energy storage devices.<a title="Select to navigate to reference" href="#cit5"><sup><span class="sup_ref">58</span></sup></a> Among the many candidates, sodium-ion batteries (SIBs), with similar storage principles and cell configurations to LIBs, have attracted widespread attention because of their abundance and low price.<a title="Select to navigate to reference" href="#cit9"><sup><span class="sup_ref">913</span></sup></a> Traditional Li<small><sup>+</sup></small> electrode materials cannot be directly applied to Na<small><sup>+</sup></small> batteries owing to their higher potential and larger ionic radius of Na<small><sup>+</sup></small> than those of Li<small><sup>+</sup></small>. Therefore, it is highly desirable to develop appropriate materials for storing Na<small><sup>+</sup></small>.<a title="Select to navigate to reference" href="#cit14"><sup><span class="sup_ref">14,15</span></sup></a></span>
<p class="otherpara">In recent years, transition metal chalcogenides have received considerable attention owing to their controllable nanostructures and excellent sodium storage performance.<a title="Select to navigate to reference" href="#cit16"><sup><span class="sup_ref">1619</span></sup></a> Compared with metal sulphides, metal selenides have higher electronic conductivity because Se is more conductive than S, and the metalselenide bonds break more easily in the conversion reaction with sodium, which leads to superior rate capability and high reversible capacity.<a title="Select to navigate to reference" href="#cit17"><sup><span class="sup_ref">17,20,21</span></sup></a> Copper selenides, anisotropic p-type semiconductor materials, are used in gas sensors, solar cells, and electrochemical energy storage because of their abundance, low cost and high capacity.<a title="Select to navigate to reference" href="#cit16"><sup><span class="sup_ref">16,2224</span></sup></a> Cu<small><sub>2<span class="italic">x</span></sub></small>Se was applied to the anode of SIBs in 2013, and it showed a specific capacity of 120 mA h g<small><sup>1</sup></small> after 100 cycles.<a title="Select to navigate to references" href="#cit25"><sup><span class="sup_ref">25</span></sup></a> To improve the electrochemical performances of copper selenides, downsizing the particle size or compositing with carbonaceous materials could be effective strategies. For example, mesoporous Cu<small><sub>2<span class="italic">x</span></sub></small>Se nanocrystals and ultrathin CuSe nanosheets, synthesized by a solvothermal reaction, delivered a capacity of 212.4 mA h g<small><sup>1</sup></small> after 3000 cycles at 5 A g<small><sup>1</sup></small> and 228 mA h g<small><sup>1</sup></small> after 10<img class="charmap" src="https://www.rsc.org/images/entities/char_2009.gif" alt="[thin space (1/6-em)]">000 cycles at 20 A g<small><sup>1</sup></small>, respectively.<a title="Select to navigate to reference" href="#cit17"><sup><span class="sup_ref">17,22</span></sup></a> By applying the liquid phase and high temperature treatment, the as-prepared nanosheet-assembled CuSe Crystal pillars and multi-heteroatom doped Cu<small><sub>1.8</sub></small>Se/C composites presented a capacity retention of 95.6% after 1200 cycles at 5 A g<small><sup>1</sup></small> (295 mA h g<small><sup>1</sup></small> at 10 A g<small><sup>1</sup></small>) and 92.6% after 600 cycles at 1 A g<small><sup>1</sup></small> (213.3 mA h g<small><sup>1</sup></small> at 0.8 A g<small><sup>1</sup></small>).<a title="Select to navigate to reference" href="#cit16"><sup><span class="sup_ref">16,26</span></sup></a> However, these strategies mostly use the dangerous and expensive reagents (hydrazine hydrate or sodium borohydride) or high-temperature treatment, thus increasing the production difficulty and cost.</p>
<p class="otherpara">Herein, we report a facile synthesis of two-dimensional phase-pure CuSe nanosheets <span class="italic">via</span> a simple hydrothermal reaction using inexpensive and readily available reagents. The CuSe electrode for SIB demonstrated high initial coulombic efficiency, superior rate performance and long cycle life. In the sodium-ion hybrid capacitor with the two-dimensional CuSe nanosheets and Ti<small><sub>3</sub></small>C<small><sub>2</sub></small>T<small><sub><span class="italic">x</span></sub></small> MXene, the nanosheets without any pre-sodiation exhibited excellent energy-storage properties with respect to high energy density (63.4 W h kg<small><sup>1</sup></small> at 459.1 W kg<small><sup>1</sup></small>) and 77.7% retention of its initial capacity with 2000 cycles (at 2 A g<small><sup>1</sup></small>, 03.3 V).</p>

<h2 id="sect340"><span class="a_heading">2. Experimental section</span></h2>
<h3 id="sect343"><span class="b_heading">2.1 Material preparation</span></h3>
<span>Selenium (Se) powder (99.9%, metal basis), sodium hydroxide (NaOH, 97%) powder, copper nitrate trihydrate (Cu(NO<small><sub>3</sub></small>)<small><sub>2</sub></small>3H<small><sub>2</sub></small>O, 99.9%) and hexadecyl trimethyl ammonium bromide (CTAB, 99%) were purchased from Aladdin Industrial Co., Ltd. (Shanghai) and used without further purification. The Ti<small><sub>3</sub></small>C<small><sub>2</sub></small>T<small><sub><span class="italic">x</span></sub></small> MXene was prepared by etching Ti<small><sub>3</sub></small>AlC<small><sub>2</sub></small> powder using hydrofluoric acid, and the Ti<small><sub>3</sub></small>AlC<small><sub>2</sub></small> powder (200 mesh) was purchased from Enwang New Materials Technology Co., Ltd. The schematic diagram for the synthesis of CuSe nanosheets is shown in <a title="Select to navigate to scheme" href="#imgsch1">Scheme 1</a>. First, 0.28 g Se powder and 9.33 g NaOH were dissolved in 30 mL deionized water and stirred in a water bath at 80 C for 30 min as solution A. 0.48 g Cu(NO<small><sub>3</sub></small>)<small><sub>2</sub></small>3H<small><sub>2</sub></small>O and 0.75 g CTAB were dissolved in 15 mL deionized water to form solution B. Solution B was slowly added to solution A under violent stirring. Then, the mixture was transferred to a Teflon-lined stainless-steel autoclave with a capacity of 100 mL and reacted at 120 C for 0.5 h. After the reaction was completed, the CuSe nanosheets were obtained by washing with deionized water and drying in a vacuum oven at 60 C for 12 h.</span>

<h3 id="sect364"><span class="b_heading">2.2 Materials characterization</span></h3>
<span>The microstructures and morphologies of the samples were characterized by scanning electron microscopy (SEM, Hitachi S-4800), field emission transmission electron microscopy (TEM, FEI Tecnai G2 F20), and atomic force microscopy (AFM, MultiMode8, Bruker). The crystal structures and composites were obtained using an X-ray powder diffractometer (XRD, Bruker D2 PHASER) with Cu-K (<span class="italic"></span> = 1.5418 ) radiation. The chemical elements were analyzed using an X-ray photoelectron spectroscopy (XPS, PerkinElmer PHI 5600 XPS system) with a resolution of 0.30.5 eV from a monochromated aluminum anode X-ray source. The BrunauerEmmettTeller (BET) method was utilized to determine the surface area and pore size of the materials on the ASAP2460 2 MP at 77 K. A Raman spectrometer was carried out using an INVIA Raman microprobe (Renishaw Instruments) with a 532 nm laser source and a 50 objective lens. Fourier transform infrared (FTIR) spectra were obtained using a Thermo Nicolet NEXUS 670 unit in a transmission mode.</span>

<h3 id="sect369"><span class="b_heading">2.3 Electrochemical measurements</span></h3>
<span>The electrochemical characteristics of the CuSe nanosheets were evaluated in CR2032-type coin cells assembled in an argon-filled glove box with water and oxygen contents below 0.1 ppm. Active materials, conductive acetylene black, and carboxy methyl cellulose sodium (CMC) with a weight ratio of 7<img class="charmap" src="https://www.rsc.org/images/entities/char_2009.gif" alt="[thin space (1/6-em)]">:<img class="charmap" src="https://www.rsc.org/images/entities/char_2009.gif" alt="[thin space (1/6-em)]">2<img class="charmap" src="https://www.rsc.org/images/entities/char_2009.gif" alt="[thin space (1/6-em)]">:<img class="charmap" src="https://www.rsc.org/images/entities/char_2009.gif" alt="[thin space (1/6-em)]">1 in deionized water were mixed to form a consistent slurry, which was then cast onto copper foil substrates. After drying at 60 C for 12 h in a vacuum oven, the working electrodes were obtained by punching the foil into discs with a diameter of 12 mm. Furthermore, the working electrode was assembled with a handmade sodium foil as the counter and reference electrodes, a glass fiber (Whatman GF/F) as the separator, and 1.0 M sodium trifluomethanesulfonate (NaSO<small><sub>3</sub></small>CF<small><sub>3</sub></small>) in diglyme (DGM) as the electrolyte together for half cells. In sodium-ion hybrid capacitors, the Ti<small><sub>3</sub></small>C<small><sub>2</sub></small>T<small><sub><span class="italic">x</span></sub></small> MXene was used as the cathode material. The cathode was prepared by a similar procedure to that of the CuSe nanosheets except on an aluminum foil. Finally, sodium-ion hybrid capacitors were fabricated using the CuSe nanosheets and Ti<small><sub>3</sub></small>C<small><sub>2</sub></small>T<small><sub><span class="italic">x</span></sub></small> MXene electrodes as the battery-type and capacitor-type electrodes, respectively. The mass ratio of the cathode/anode materials was approximately 1.5. Cyclic voltammetry (CV) curves at different scanning speeds were measured and performed on a VMP3 electrochemical station. Electrochemical impedance spectroscopy (EIS) was tested in a frequency range of 100 kHz0.01 Hz with an amplitude of 5 mV. The galvanostatic charge/discharge (GCD) measurements, cycling and rate properties were monitored using a Neware battery test system at ambient temperature.</span>


<h2 id="sect383"><span class="a_heading">3. Results and discussions</span></h2>
<h3 id="sect386"><span class="b_heading">3.1 Syntheses and characterization</span></h3>
<span>As shown in the SEM images (<a title="Select to navigate to figure" href="#imgfig1">Fig. 1ac</a>), the nanosheets are uniformly distributed without stacking and large-area agglomeration. The thickness of the CuSe nanosheets was characterized <span class="italic">via</span> AFM. The results show that the thickness is about 6 nm (Fig. S1<a title="Select to navigate to footnote" href="#fn1"></a>), which indicates that two-dimensional CuSe nanosheets possess a large effective surface area, abundant electrochemically active sites and a convenient ion transportation path.<a title="Select to navigate to reference" href="#cit16"><sup><span class="sup_ref">16,17,22</span></sup></a> BET was used to analyze the pore size and specific surface area of CuSe nanosheets (Fig. S2<a title="Select to navigate to footnote" href="#fn1"></a>), in which the pore size is about 4 nm and the specific surface area is 2.88 m<small><sup>2</sup></small> g<small><sup>1</sup></small>. The elemental composition of CuSe is analyzed <span class="italic">via</span> energy dispersive X-ray analysis (Fig. S3<a title="Select to navigate to footnote" href="#fn1"></a>), which shows that the stoichiometric ratio of Cu to Se is approximately 1<img class="charmap" src="https://www.rsc.org/images/entities/char_2009.gif" alt="[thin space (1/6-em)]">:<img class="charmap" src="https://www.rsc.org/images/entities/char_2009.gif" alt="[thin space (1/6-em)]">1. <p class="otherpara"><a title="Select to navigate to figure" href="#imgfig3">Fig. 3b</a> displays the chargedischarge curves of CuSe nanosheets at 0.1 A g<small><sup>1</sup></small>. Two discharge platforms exist at 1.64 and 0.66 V in the discharge curve, and the charging platforms are at 1.48 and 1.95 V, which are consistent with the results of CV. These results demonstrate the prominent rate capability and cycle performance of CuSe nanosheets for SIBs.</p>

<p class="otherpara">To reveal the reaction kinetics and superior performance of CuSe nanosheets, CV curves at different scan rates are recorded (<a title="Select to navigate to figure" href="#imgfig4">Fig. 4a</a>), in which the CV profiles display a similar curve shape, indicating excellent rate performance and reversible sodium storage capability of CuSe nanosheets. According to the existing reports,<a title="Select to navigate to references" href="#cit38"><sup><span class="sup_ref">38</span></sup></a> the peak current (<span class="italic">i</span>) of the pseudocapacitive contribution is proportional to the sweep rate (<span class="italic">v</span>), while the current associated with the diffusion control program is proportional to the quadrat root of the sweep rate. Therefore, the qualitative deduction for the charge storage mechanism can be obtained using the following formula:<a title="Select to navigate to references" href="#cit39"><sup><span class="sup_ref">39</span></sup></a></p><table><tbody><tr><td class="leftEqn">&nbsp;</td></tr><tr><td class="eqn"><span id="eqn4">
<span id="eqn4"><span class="italic">i</span> = <span class="italic">av</span><small><sup><span class="italic">b</span></sup></small></span>
</span></td><td class="rightEqn">(4)</td></tr></tbody></table>where <span class="italic">a</span> is a constant, and <span class="italic">b</span> depends on the slopes of log(<span class="italic">i</span>) <span class="italic">versus</span> log(<span class="italic">v</span>). When the value of <span class="italic">b</span> is 0.5, the electrode material for the charge storage is dominated by the diffusion-controlled process. The <span class="italic">b</span> value close to 1.0 represents the pseudocapacitive-dominated process, ranging from 0.5 to 1, indicating the combination of the diffusion-controlled process with the pseudocapacitive-dominated process.<a title="Select to navigate to references" href="#cit40"><sup><span class="sup_ref">40</span></sup></a> As shown in <a title="Select to navigate to figure" href="#imgfig4">Fig. 4b</a>, the obtained <span class="italic">b</span> values are 0.72 and 0.74, respectively, suggesting that the reaction is controlled by the combination of the diffusion-controlled behavior with the pseudocapacitive dominated behavior. Furthermore, electrochemical impedance spectroscopy (EIS) was conducted (<a title="Select to navigate to figure" href="#imgfig5">Fig. 5a</a>), in which the large angle (83.1) between the straight line and <span class="italic">Z</span>-axis suggests the dominant pseudocapacitive behavior.<p></p>

<h2 id="sect845"><span class="a_heading">4. Conclusions</span></h2>
<span>In summary, two-dimensional phase-pure CuSe nanosheets were successfully synthesized by a facile and simple hydrothermal reaction using inexpensive and easily available reagents. Benefiting from the high conductivity and morphological characteristics of two-dimensional CuSe nanosheets with a large effective surface area, abundant electrochemically active sites and a convenient ion transportation path, the CuSe electrode for SIBs exhibited high-rate capability and long-term stability. Furthermore, CuSe nanosheets for sodium-ion hybrid capacitors also resulted in the desired energy density (63.4 W h kg<small><sup>1</sup></small> at 459.1 W kg<small><sup>1</sup></small>) and relatively long cycle life (77.7% capacitance retention with 2000 cycles at 2 A g<small><sup>1</sup></small>).</span>
