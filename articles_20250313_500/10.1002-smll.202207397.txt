Herein, density functional theory simulations for the first time optimize MoS<sub>2</sub> interlayer distance between 0.80 and 1.01 nm for sodium storage. 1‐Butyl‐3‐methyl‐imidazolium acetate ([BMIm]Ac) induces cellulose oligomers to intercalate MoS<sub>2</sub> interlayers for achieving controllable distance by changing the mass ratio of cellulose to [BMIm]Ac. Based on these findings, porous carbon loading the interlayer‐expanded MoS<sub>2</sub> allowing Na<sup>+</sup> to insert with fast kinetics is synthesized. A carbon layer derived from [BMIm]Ac and cellulose coating the composite prevents the MoS<sub>2</sub> from contacting electrolytes, leading to less sulfur loss for a more reversible specific capacity. Meanwhile, MoS<sub>2</sub> and carbon have a strong interfacial connection through MoN binding, contributing to enhanced structural stability. As expected, while cycling 250 times at 0.1 A g<sup>‐1</sup>, the MoS<sub>2</sub>‐porous carbon composite displays an optimal reversible capacity at 517.79 mAh g<sup>‐1</sup> as a sodium‐ion batteries anode. The cyclic test of 1.0 A g<sup>‐1</sup> also shows considerable stability (310.74 mAh g<sup>‐1</sup> after 1000 cycles with 86.26% retentive capacity). This study will open up new possibilities of modifying MoS<sub>2</sub> that serves as an applicable material as sodium‐ion battery anode.</p></abstract><abstract xml:id="smll202207397-abs-0001" type="graphical" xml:lang="en"><p>The optimal MoS<sub>2</sub> interlayer distance for sodium storage is revealed by theory simulation and MoS<sub>2</sub> interlayer distance is controllably expanded by increased cellulose dosage with ionic liquids. Based on these findings, interlayer‐expanded MoS<sub>2</sub> is coated with interfacial connection of MoN bonds, it shows high reversible specific capacity and outstanding cyclic stability.<blockFixed type="graphic"><mediaResourceGroup><mediaResource href="urn:x-wiley:16136810:media:smll202207397:smll202207397-gra-0001" alt="image"/><mediaResource mimeType="image/png" href="graphic/smll202207397-gra-0001-m.png" rendition="webHiRes" alt="image"/></mediaResourceGroup></blockFixed></p></abstract></abstractGroup></contentMeta></header><body xml:id="smll202207397-body-0001" sectionsNumbered="yes"><section xml:id="smll202207397-sec-0010"><title type="main">Introduction</title><p>Sodium‐ion batteries (SIBs) had expectations in surpassing lithium‐ion batteries (LIBs) by reason of plentiful sodium resources, safety, and comparable working mechanism. However, Na<sup>+</sup> (1.02 Å) was bigger than Li<sup>+</sup> (0.76 Å), leading to graphite (0.34 nm interlayer distance), used as the most common anode for LIBs, could not reversibly intercalate in SIBs. Molybdenum disulfide (MoS<sub>2</sub>), regarded as a conversion‐type anode material with a high theoretical capacity (670 mAh g<sup>−1</sup>) ideal for SIBs, has caught the interest of numerous researchers. The use of MoS<sub>2</sub> for SIB anode was nonetheless constrained by its low intrinsic conductivity and limited Na<sup>+</sup> diffusion in the interlayers, further resulting in inefficient utilization of the material with poor capacity.<sup>[</sup><link href="#smll202207397-bib-0001 #smll202207397-bib-0002"><sup>1,2</sup></link><sup>]</sup> Numerous attempts have been carried out to expand MoS<sub>2</sub> interlayer to address these shortcomings. On one hand, the top‐down strategy was applied to introduce molecules into the MoS<sub>2</sub> interlayer to increase the spacing. Poly(ethylene oxide) was used to expand the interlayers from 0.62 to 1.45 nm by intercalating MoS<sub>2</sub>, achieving 225 mAh g<sup>−1</sup> at 0.05 A g<sup>−1</sup> for SIBs.<sup>[</sup><link href="#smll202207397-bib-0003"><sup>3</sup></link><sup>]</sup> By employing small molecules intercalation such as dimethylacetamide and 4, 4′‐bipyridine, the MoS<sub>2</sub> interlayer distance was adjusted about to 0.98 or 1.24 nm with high crystallinity, which raised the electrical conductivity from 1.3 × 10<sup>−4</sup> to 3.5 × 10<sup>−3</sup> or 8.9 × 10<sup>−4</sup> S cm<sup>−1</sup> and enhanced the capacity from 195 to 465 or 310 mAh g<sup>−1</sup> after 600 cycles at 0.1 A g<sup>−1</sup> for SIBs.<sup>[</sup><link href="#smll202207397-bib-0004"><sup>4</sup></link><sup>]</sup> The MoS<sub>2</sub> interlayer distance could be readily increased by this strategy for improving Na<sup>+</sup> kinetics, but it was challenging to precisely control the interlayer distance. There has been no report found yet of how to controllably expand the MoS<sub>2</sub> interlayer. Furthermore, the interlayer distance in MoS<sub>2</sub> for optimal sodium storage has rarely been reported by now. On the other hand, the bottom‐up approaches were used to synthesize the interlayer‐extended MoS<sub>2</sub> by heteroatom doping or phase control.<sup>[</sup><link href="#smll202207397-bib-0005 #smll202207397-bib-0006 #smll202207397-bib-0007"><sup>5–7</sup></link><sup>]</sup> But these approaches were difficult to apply and the variation of interlayer distance was very limited.</p><p>The expanded interlayer allowed more Na<sup>+</sup> inserted in MoS<sub>2</sub> with fast kinetics, which led to sulfur loss and huge volume expansion.<sup>[</sup><link href="#smll202207397-bib-0008 #smll202207397-bib-0009"><sup>8,9</sup></link><sup>]</sup> Partially MoS<sub>2</sub> became unrenewable when the polysulfide was created during the conversion process diffused in the electrolyte.<sup>[</sup><link href="#smll202207397-bib-0010"><sup>10</sup></link><sup>]</sup> Large amounts of Na<sup>+</sup> insertion triggered significant internal structure strain that caused the volume expansion. As a result, electrode materials would suffer huge structural damage with capacity loss during the processes of charge/discharge. In response to this, MoS<sub>2</sub> has been combined with carbon materials to alleviate sulfur loss by avoiding direct contact between MoS<sub>2</sub> and electrolytes as well as to reduce volume expansion.<sup>[</sup><link href="#smll202207397-bib-0011 #smll202207397-bib-0012 #smll202207397-bib-0013"><sup>11–13</sup></link><sup>]</sup> Porous carbon (PC) was an ideal carbon matrix due to its turbostratic structure and a significant amount of porosity. The former contributed to sufficient stability and conductivity, while the latter provided active sites to attach MoS<sub>2</sub>. But MoO<sub>4</sub><sup>−</sup> and Mo<sub>7</sub>O<sub>24</sub><sup>6−</sup> were usually used as MoS<sub>2</sub> precursors, which led to electrostatic repulsion with the negatively charged carbon, resulting in weak interfacial interaction.<sup>[</sup><link href="#smll202207397-bib-0002"><sup>2</sup></link><sup>]</sup></p><p>Ionic liquids (ILs) were eco‐friendly organic solvents that were almost completely nonvolatile and had excellent capabilities of dissolution and penetration. In our prior investigation, Imidazolium‐based ILs were used to induce carbon structures for controllable functionalization. In the carbonization process of microcrystalline cellulose (MCC), crystalline carbon was created with the imidazole rings acting as soft template, which realized highly graphitization of MCC derived carbon at low temperatures and rich nitrogen doping.<sup>[</sup><link href="#smll202207397-bib-0014 #smll202207397-bib-0015"><sup>14,15</sup></link><sup>]</sup> Moreover, it has been used to build stable interfacial connections by forming strong covalent bonding.<sup>[</sup><link href="#smll202207397-bib-0016 #smll202207397-bib-0017"><sup>16,17</sup></link><sup>]</sup> ILs and MCC in mixture depolymerized to oligomers in solution at different concentrations depending on the mass of MCC added. Based on the findings, a hypothesis could be proposed that the interlayer distance of MoS<sub>2</sub> was controllable by changing the concentration of oligomers for the intercalation of MoS<sub>2</sub>. The oligomers could easily enter the MoS<sub>2</sub> interlayer with ILs of strong permeability, and the expansion effect was enhanced by increasing the concentration of MCC.</p><p>In this study, by using density functional theory (DFT) simulations, the optimal interlayer distance of MoS<sub>2</sub> for sodium storage, was revealed to be in the range of 0.80–1.01 nm for the first time, and 1‐butyl‐3‐methyl‐imidazolium acetate ([BMIm]Ac) induced MCC to controllably expand MoS<sub>2</sub> interlayers by changing the mass ratio of MCC to [BMIm]Ac. Furthermore, a MoS<sub>2</sub>‐carbon composite with an interlayer‐expanded feature was synthesized. After MoS<sub>2</sub> was loaded on the porous carbon, the [BMIm]Ac induced cellulose oligomers to intercalate the MoS<sub>2</sub> interlayers for the optimal distance. The electrochemical performances of the MoS<sub>2</sub>‐carbon composites as SIB anodes were systematically investigated to verify the hypothesis.</p></section><section xml:id="smll202207397-sec-0020"><title type="main">Results and Discussion</title><p>The interlayer distance of MoS<sub>2</sub> was evaluated by X‐ray diffraction (XRD) as shown in <b>Figure</b> <link href="#smll202207397-fig-0001"><b>1</b>a</link>. The interlayer distances of all the samples calculated based on XRD results were listed in <b>Table</b> <link href="#smll202207397-tbl-0001"><b>1</b></link> for comparison. The MoS<sub>2</sub> produced by the hydrothermal process showed a sharp (002) peak at 14.34° consistent with an average interlayer distance at 0.62 nm calculated by the Bragg equation. There was no shift for the first peak of E‐MoS<sub>2</sub>‐1's pattern, indicating the addition of [BMIm]Ac alone could not expand the interlayers of MoS<sub>2</sub>. In addition, the extra sharp peaks at 32.13°, 35.01°, 48.51°, and 84.02° represented the existence of crystalline MoO<sub>3</sub>, while the extra sharp peaks of 64.13° and 75.51° suggested crystalline Mo<sub>2</sub>N, which were reaction products of [BMIm]Ac and MoS<sub>2</sub>. By comparing the results of Table <link href="#smll202207397-tbl-0001">1</link>, the interlayer distance of MoS<sub>2</sub> apparently increased with increased mass ratio of MCC to [BMIm]Ac. Cellulose oligomers derived from the degradation of MCC during the calcination inserted into MoS<sub>2</sub> interlayers leading to the expansion of MoS<sub>2</sub> interlayers. With the addition of more MCC, and the oligomer concentration kept increasing which was attributed to the enhanced expansion of MoS<sub>2</sub> interlayers. As a result, the first diffraction peak gradually shifted from 14.34° to 8.98° corresponding to the interlayers kept being expended from 0.62 to 1.01 nm. No significant further shifting could be observed by increasing the mass ratio of MCC to MoS<sub>2</sub> over 0.6 or changing the reaction temperature. In the XRD pattern of E‐MoS<sub>2</sub>‐9, all diffraction peaks became sharp and the peaks at 35.87°, 44.15°, 60.14°, and 72.79° reappeared suggesting a high crystallinity for MoS<sub>2</sub>, which was attributed to the highest reaction temperature. For imaging the morphology of MoS<sub>2</sub>, a high‐resolution transmission electron microscope (HRTEM) was used. The average interlayer distances of MoS<sub>2</sub>, E‐MoS<sub>2</sub>‐2, E‐MoS<sub>2</sub>‐3, E‐MoS<sub>2</sub>‐4, E‐MoS<sub>2</sub>‐5, and E‐MoS<sub>2</sub>‐6 were calculated to be 0.62, 0.66, 0.75, 0.84, 0.92, and 1.01 nm, respectively, as displayed in Figure <link href="#smll202207397-fig-0001">1b–g</link>. By comparing the Table <link href="#smll202207397-tbl-0001">1</link> and Figure <link href="#smll202207397-fig-0001">1</link>, the results of calculated by HRTEM were highly consistent with those measured by XRD. Remarkably, controllable expansion of MoS<sub>2</sub> interlayers could be achieved by controlling the mass ratio of MCC to [BMIm]Ac. It was also important to note that the HRTEM images demonstrated that MoS<sub>2</sub> progressively tended to be disordered as the interlayer distance increased, which corresponded to a gradually illustration was shown in <b>Figure</b> <link href="#smll202207397-fig-0002"><b>2</b>a</link>. Since cellulose was initially dissolved in [BMIm]Ac, its intermolecular hydrogen bonds have been destroyed, which induced the MCC to break easily into oligomers. Then [BMIm]Ac with strong permeability penetrated into the MoS<sub>2</sub> interlayers with the oligomers. The thermally stable imidazole rings of [BMIm]Ac then attracted the oligomers in the MoS<sub>2</sub> interlayers for carbon recombination during the pyrolysis process. The more cellulose was added, the more oligomers were produced to insert the MoS<sub>2</sub> interlayers, allowing for controllably expanding MoS<sub>2</sub> interlayers. However, it became urgent to know the optimal interlayer distance of MoS<sub>2</sub> for sodium storage. Herein, DFT was used to supply fundamental calculations for revealing an optimal range. The adsorption energy (<i>E</i><sub>ads</sub>) for sodium ion, was calculated using Equation (<link href="#smll202207397-disp-0001">1</link>)<displayedItem xml:id="smll202207397-disp-0001" type="mathematics"><label>1</label><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" display="block" altimg="urn:x-wiley:dummy:media:smll202207397:smll202207397-math-0001" wiley:location="graphic/smll202207397-math-0001.png"><semantics><mrow><mtable><mtr><mtd><mrow><msub><mi>E</mi><mtext>ads</mtext></msub><mo linebreak="badbreak">=</mo><msub><mi>E</mi><mtext>ion+surface</mtext></msub><mo linebreak="goodbreak">−</mo><msub><mi>E</mi><mtext>surface</mtext></msub><mo linebreak="goodbreak">−</mo><msub><mi>E</mi><mtext>ion</mtext></msub></mrow></mtd></mtr></mtable></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\[\begin{array}{*{20}{c}}{{E_{{\rm{ads}}}} = {E_{{\rm{ion + surface}}}} - {E_{{\rm{surface}}}} - {E_{{\rm{ion}}}}}\end{array}\]</annotation></semantics></math></displayedItem>where <i>E</i><sub>surface</sub> was the energy of the two‐layer MoS<sub>2</sub>, <i>E</i><sub>ion</sub> represented the energy of Na<sup>+</sup>, and <i>E</i><sub>ion+surface</sub> represented the adsorbed system total energy. Na<sup>+</sup> adsorbed on the lower MoS<sub>2</sub> layer surface at 1.01 nm distance, and the adsorption energy tended to be constant. MoS<sub>2</sub> interlayers were controllably expanded by increasing the mass ratio of MCC to [BMIm]Ac, and a MoS<sub>2</sub>‐carbon composite of porous carbon loading interlayer‐expanded MoS<sub>2</sub> was synthesized. As the anode of SIBs testing at 0.1 A g<sup>−1</sup>, PC@MoS<sub>2</sub>‐4 showed 517.79 mAh g<sup>−1</sup> after 250 cycles and significant stability after 1000 cycles (310.74 mAh g<sup>−1</sup> with 86.26% retentive capacity) at the cyclic test of 1.0 A g<sup>−1</sup>. The interlayer expansion of MoS<sub>2</sub> improved Na<sup>+</sup> diffusion and charge transport. [BMIm]Ac and MCC formed a carbon layer coating the MoS<sub>2</sub> to prevent polysulfide diffusing in electrolytes during the process of MoS<sub>2</sub> sodiation. The reversible specific capacity was increased due to less sulfur loss. MoN bonds formed between MoS<sub>2</sub> and carbon contributed to strong interfacial connections to weaken structural strain and volume expansion, resulting in great cycle stability. This discovery will open up new possibilities and serve as inspiration for the MoS<sub>2</sub> anode in the future.</p></section><section xml:id="smll202207397-sec-0040"><title type="main">Experimental Section</title><section xml:id="smll202207397-sec-0050" numbered="no"><section xml:id="smll202207397-sec-0060" numbered="no"><section xml:id="smll202207397-sec-0070" numbered="no"><title type="main">Materials</title><p>Jiangshan Lvyi Bamboo Charcoal Co.
