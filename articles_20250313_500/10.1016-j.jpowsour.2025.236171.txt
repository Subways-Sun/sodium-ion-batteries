                  Layered germanium phosphide (GeP) is considered a promising anode material for lithium-ion batteries (LIBs) and sodium-ion batteries (SIBs) due to its high specific capacity and rapid Li+/Na+ diffusion. Herein, we first reveal that the pristine GeP nanosheet shows ∼110 % and 128 % volume expansion during the first lithiation/sodiation by in-situ transmission electron microscopy (TEM), respectively, which leads to severe detectable fractures or cracks of the GeP anode. Thus, we revisit the failure mechanism of the GeP anode is attributed to the unsatisfactory mechanical robustness. Then, we propose a simple and efficient strategy that combines GeP nanosheets with single-walled carbon nanotubes (SWCNTs) to design a GeP/SWCNTs composite anode. Nanoindentation experiments and theoretical calculations confirm that the GeP/SWCNTs composite exhibits reduced internal stress to alleviate expansion in the first lithiation/sodiation. As a result, the GeP/SWCNTs composite anode delivers an initial Coulombic efficiency of ∼85 % and ∼90 % in LIBs and SIBs, respectively. More importantly, the specific capacity retentions are improved from ∼11 % and ∼8 % after 50 cycles to ∼50 % and ∼51 % remarkably after 200 cycles in LIBs and SIBs, respectively.
