                  Sodium-ion batteries (SIBs) have gained prominence as the ideal choice for next-generation large-scale energy storage systems owing to advantages such as sodium abundance and cost-effectiveness. However, their synthesis requires harsh conditions such as high pressure and temperature, and the cathode materials exhibit poor electrical conductivity and sluggish kinetics, hindering SIBs from meeting the requirements for practical applications. In this study, a conversion-type Gr/FeSO4 nanocomposite based on earth-abundant and environmentally friendly elements was developed using a high-energy mechanical ball-milling method. The conversion reaction in FeSO4 was triggered via a simple direct-contact pre-sodiation method, wherein the FeSO4 particles were reversibly converted to Fe0 and Na2SO4 and recovered in the next charging process. Meanwhile, graphene in Gr/FeSO4 acted as a conductive matrix that provided additional electron transfer paths, resulting in increased ionic and electronic conductivities and enhanced diffusion kinetics. Owing to these matrices, the as-prepared Gr/FeSO4 nanocomposite exhibited remarkable long-term cyclability. This study highlights the advantages of combining pre-sodiation and conversion reactions for cathodes and demonstrates the feasibility of employing naturally abundant FeSO4 for high-performance SIBs.

            <ce:abstract class="author" id="ab010" view="all"><ce:abstract-sec id="as010" view="all">
                  <ce:simple-para id="sp0010" view="all">Sodium-ion batteries (SIBs) have gained prominence as the ideal choice for next-generation large-scale energy storage systems owing to advantages such as sodium abundance and cost-effectiveness. However, their synthesis requires harsh conditions such as high pressure and temperature, and the cathode materials exhibit poor electrical conductivity and sluggish kinetics, hindering SIBs from meeting the requirements for practical applications. In this study, a conversion-type Gr/FeSO<ce:inf loc="post">4</ce:inf> nanocomposite based on earth-abundant and environmentally friendly elements was developed using a high-energy mechanical ball-milling method. The conversion reaction in FeSO<ce:inf loc="post">4</ce:inf> was triggered via a simple direct-contact pre-sodiation method, wherein the FeSO<ce:inf loc="post">4</ce:inf> particles were reversibly converted to Fe<ce:sup loc="post">0</ce:sup> and Na<ce:inf loc="post">2</ce:inf>SO<ce:inf loc="post">4</ce:inf> and recovered in the next charging process. Meanwhile, graphene in Gr/FeSO<ce:inf loc="post">4</ce:inf> acted as a conductive matrix that provided additional electron transfer paths, resulting in increased ionic and electronic conductivities and enhanced diffusion kinetics. Owing to these matrices, the as-prepared Gr/FeSO<ce:inf loc="post">4</ce:inf> nanocomposite exhibited remarkable long-term cyclability. This study highlights the advantages of combining pre-sodiation and conversion reactions for cathodes and demonstrates the feasibility of employing naturally abundant FeSO<ce:inf loc="post">4</ce:inf> for high-performance SIBs.</ce:simple-para>

                  <ce:section-title id="st025">Introduction</ce:section-title>
                  <ce:para id="p0030" view="all">Because of their cost-effectiveness and the abundance of the constituent materials, sodium-ion batteries (SIBs) are currently being developed as alternatives to commercialized lithium-ion batteries (LIBs) for large-scale applications <ce:cross-refs refid="b0005 b0010 b0015 b0020 b0025" id="r0005">[1â5]</ce:cross-refs>. Several cutting-edge studies have reported high-performance electrodes composed of earth-abundant and inexpensive elements for application in SIBs <ce:cross-refs refid="b0025 b0030 b0035 b0040 b0045 b0050" id="r0010">[5â10]</ce:cross-refs>. In this regard, Fe exhibits numerous advantages as an electrode material, such as cost-effectiveness owing to its natural abundance, simple refining process, and safety, and has attracted significant attention for enhancing the electrochemical activity of electrochemical energy storage systems <ce:cross-refs refid="b0055 b0060" id="r0015">[11,12]</ce:cross-refs>. Owing to their superior chemical characteristics, significant studies have been conducted on Fe-based electrode materials. However, the Fe-based materials currently used in SIBs experience volume changes and exhibit low conductivities, similar to those used in LIBs <ce:cross-refs refid="b0030 b0045 b0065" id="r0020">[6,9,13]</ce:cross-refs>. Therefore, numerous techniques used for LIBs have been employed to address these problems in SIBs, including nano-sizing and the fabrication of composite materials <ce:cross-refs refid="b0060 b0070" id="r0025">[12,14]</ce:cross-refs>.</ce:para>
                  <ce:para id="p0035" view="all">To date, various Fe-based cathode materials have been reported for SIBs, such as, layered oxides, PBA cathodes, and polyanionic compounds <ce:cross-refs refid="b0060 b0065 b0070 b0075 b0080 b0085 b0090" id="r0030">[12â18]</ce:cross-refs>. It has been observed that energy densities of layered oxides are greater than those of polyanionic compounds and PBAs, and this energy in the layered oxides, <ce:italic>e.g.,</ce:italic> Na<ce:inf loc="post">0.67</ce:inf>Mn<ce:inf loc="post">0.5</ce:inf>Fe<ce:inf loc="post">0.5</ce:inf>O<ce:inf loc="post">2,</ce:inf> NaMn<ce:inf loc="post">0.33</ce:inf>Fe<ce:inf loc="post">0.33</ce:inf>Ni<ce:inf loc="post">0.33</ce:inf>O<ce:inf loc="post">2,</ce:inf> Na<ce:inf loc="post">0.75</ce:inf>(Cu<ce:inf loc="post">0.1</ce:inf>Fe<ce:inf loc="post">0.1</ce:inf>Mn<ce:inf loc="post">0.8</ce:inf>)O<ce:inf loc="post">2,</ce:inf> Na<ce:inf loc="post">0.76</ce:inf>Ni<ce:inf loc="post">0.33</ce:inf>Mn<ce:inf loc="post">0.5</ce:inf>Fe<ce:inf loc="post">0.1</ce:inf>Ti<ce:inf loc="post">0.07</ce:inf>O<ce:inf loc="post">2</ce:inf> etc., originates from the high capacity of up to 200 mAh g<ce:sup loc="post">â1</ce:sup>. However, these layered structures suffer from low operating potentials (<ce:italic>i.e.,</ce:italic> the Fe<ce:sup loc="post">4+</ce:sup>/Fe<ce:sup loc="post">3+</ce:sup> redox couple), high irreversible capacities, and structure disorder because of multiple phase transition, leading to voltage decay and cycling instabilities <ce:cross-refs refid="b0075 b0080 b0085" id="r0035">[15â17]</ce:cross-refs>. PBAs (Na<ce:inf loc="post">2</ce:inf>M[Fe(CN)<ce:inf loc="post">6,</ce:inf> MÂ =Â transition metals) and polyanionic compounds (pyrophosphates, fluorophosphates, and sulfates) exhibit a similar high average operating voltage (<ce:italic>i.e.,</ce:italic> 2.75â3.75Â V), but their specific capacity is less than that of layered oxides (<ce:italic>i.e.,</ce:italic> 160Â mAh.g<ce:sup loc="post">â1</ce:sup>). In PBA, the reduced coulombuc effecinecy (CE), fast capacity decay, lattice distortion due to presence of Fe(CN<ce:inf loc="post">6</ce:inf>) vacancies and water molecules leads to irreversible phase transition and structure collapse, resulting in capacity fading and a short cycling lifespan. On the other hand, the polyanionic compounds such as, Na<ce:inf loc="post">2</ce:inf>FePO<ce:inf loc="post">4</ce:inf>F, Na<ce:inf loc="post">2</ce:inf>FeP<ce:inf loc="post">2</ce:inf>O<ce:inf loc="post">7,</ce:inf> Na<ce:inf loc="post">3</ce:inf>Fe<ce:inf loc="post">2</ce:inf>(PO<ce:inf loc="post">4</ce:inf>)<ce:inf loc="post">3</ce:inf>, Na<ce:inf loc="post">4</ce:inf>Fe<ce:inf loc="post">3</ce:inf>(PO<ce:inf loc="post">4</ce:inf>)<ce:inf loc="post">2</ce:inf>(P<ce:inf loc="post">2</ce:inf>O<ce:inf loc="post">7</ce:inf>), and Na<ce:inf loc="post">3</ce:inf>Fe<ce:inf loc="post">3</ce:inf>(PO<ce:inf loc="post">4</ce:inf>)(P<ce:inf loc="post">2</ce:inf>O<ce:inf loc="post">7</ce:inf>)<ce:hsp sp="0.25"/>are considered to be the most practically promising compounds for sodium storage because of their low-cost, high operating potential, and thermal stability. However, they did not receive significant attention due to their difficult and high-temperature synthesis process <ce:cross-ref refid="b0095" id="c0060">[19]</ce:cross-ref>. Whereas, sulfate ((SO<ce:inf loc="post">4</ce:inf>)<ce:sup loc="post">2-</ce:sup>)-based polyanion compounds, such as, Fe<ce:inf loc="post">2</ce:inf>(SO<ce:inf loc="post">4</ce:inf>)<ce:inf loc="post">3,</ce:inf> and Na<ce:italic>
                        <ce:inf loc="post">x</ce:inf>
                     </ce:italic>Fe<ce:inf loc="post">2</ce:inf>(SO<ce:inf loc="post">4</ce:inf>)<ce:inf loc="post">3</ce:inf> have the advantage of high operating voltage due to their high electronegativity compared to the other polyanions. However, slow kinetics reaction of these materials is the challenge, which can be overcome by nano-sizing, conductive carbon coating, or forming a protective an interphase layer at the surface <ce:cross-refs refid="b0060 b0090" id="r0040">[12,18]</ce:cross-refs>. Modification of these Fe and sulfate ((SO<ce:inf loc="post">4</ce:inf>)<ce:sup loc="post">2-</ce:sup>)-based materials with carbonaceous materials provides distinct enhancements, such as greater ionic and electronic conductivities, larger contact area between active materials and electrolyte, adjust the significant volume changes, prevent irreversible phase transition, and therefore enhance the electrochemical performance in term of reversible capacity, rate capability, and cycle lifespan. The nanocomposites provide the following advantages: (1) enhanced storage of sodium ions in carbonaceous materials, (2) increased electronic conductivity, and (3) decreased diffusion length of Na ions via nano-sizing <ce:cross-ref refid="b0070" id="c0065">[14]</ce:cross-ref>. Therefore, carbon nanocomposites are an effective strategy for achieving novel electrode materials with significantly improved electrochemical performances compared to their nanocomposite counterparts. However, previous efforts have only focused on applying Fe-based composites as anodes in SIBs; thus far, their application as cathode materials has been lacking.</ce:para>
                  <ce:para id="p0040" view="all">Herein, for the first time, we propose a low-cost carbon- and Fe-based nanocomposite as the cathode material for SIBs. The unique nanocomposite cathode comprising graphene and FeSO<ce:inf loc="post">4</ce:inf> (hereinafter referred to as Gr/FeSO<ce:inf loc="post">4</ce:inf>) functions via a conversion reaction and exhibited a theoretical specific capacity ofÂ â¼Â 355Â mA g<ce:sup loc="post">â1</ce:sup>. We demonstrated that performing a pre-sodiation process on the Gr/FeSO<ce:inf loc="post">4</ce:inf> nanocomposite cathode enabled a reversible conversion reaction during charge/discharge cycles for practically viable SIB devices. During the pre-sodiation process, Na<ce:sup loc="post">+</ce:sup> can be directly inserted into cathode material to compensate for the deficiency of Na ions in the active material <ce:cross-ref refid="b0100" id="c0070">[20]</ce:cross-ref>. In this study, a direct-contact method of pre-sodiation is used, which is considered to be the simplest, fast, economical, and effective method, where a potential difference between the electrode and Na-metal layer produces an electromotive force for the movement of electrons. Meanwhile, Na<ce:sup loc="post">+</ce:sup> from the Na metal move to the active material and contribute to the sodium deficiency. The conversion reaction mechanism of the Gr/FeSO<ce:inf loc="post">4</ce:inf> nanocomposite was elucidated by using <ce:italic>in-situ</ce:italic> X-ray diffraction (XRD), Fe K- edge X-ray absorption spectroscopy (XAS), transmission electron microscopy (TEM), and X-ray photoelectron spectroscopy (XPS) to systematically investigate the structural and compositional changes during charging and discharging. The combined structural analyses revealed that FeSO<ce:inf loc="post">4</ce:inf> converted to Na<ce:inf loc="post">2</ce:inf>SO<ce:inf loc="post">4</ce:inf> and Fe metal upon pre-sodiation.</ce:para>

                  <ce:para id="p0080" view="all">To further investigate the conversion reaction from FeSO<ce:inf loc="post">4</ce:inf> to Na<ce:inf loc="post">2</ce:inf>SO<ce:inf loc="post">4</ce:inf>, <ce:italic>in-situ</ce:italic> XRD was conducted during electrochemical cycling, as shown in <ce:cross-ref refid="f0025" id="c0280">Figure 5</ce:cross-ref>e.                <ce:section id="s0015" view="all">
                  <ce:label>3</ce:label>
                  <ce:section-title id="st035">Conclusions</ce:section-title>
                  <ce:para id="p0085" view="all">We report a facile and low-cost method for preparing high-performance conversion-type composite electrodes for SIBs using commercially available sulphates (<ce:italic>i.e.,</ce:italic> FeSO<ce:inf loc="post">4</ce:inf>) and naturally abundant graphene. A simple direct-contact pre-sodiation method was utilized to initiate the conversion of FeSO<ce:inf loc="post">4</ce:inf> into Fe<ce:sup loc="post">0</ce:sup> and Na<ce:inf loc="post">2</ce:inf>SO<ce:inf loc="post">4</ce:inf>. The use of graphene as a conductive source significantly improved the intrinsic electrical conductivity by successfully improving the ionic and electronic diffusion kinetics during sodiation/desodiation. The improved Na diffusion and higher capacitive contribution were, in turn, responsible for the enhanced electrochemical performance of the Gr/FeSO<ce:inf loc="post">4</ce:inf> nanocomposite, which exhibited a specific capacity of 164 mAh g<ce:sup loc="post">â1</ce:sup> at 0.1C with a capacity retention of 48Â % after 100 cycles. These findings highlight the immense potential of using novel, earth-abundant, eco-friendly, and sustainable cathodes for large-scale SIBs. And therefore, despite conquering the portable electronic markets, challenges such as long-term stability, high specific energy, safety and access to cheap and cost effective and more eco-efficient electrode materials seem to be the overriding factors in high-volume applications of the technology and are needed to be addressed in the future.</ce:para>

                     <ce:section-title id="st050">Electrochemistry</ce:section-title>
                     <ce:para id="p0095" view="all">For the electrochemical tests, electrodes were prepared by mixing the Gr/FeSO<ce:inf loc="post">4</ce:inf> nanocomposite active material with the conductive agent carbon black and a polyvinylidene fluoride (PVDF) binder at a ratio of 8:1:1 (wt.%). Na/Na<ce:sup loc="post">+</ce:sup>. Low and high cut-off voltages were established at 1.0Â V and 4.0Â V, respectively, because the both the active material and electrolyte were considered to be stable in this voltage range for SIBs. Briefly, the Gr/FeSO<ce:inf loc="post">4</ce:inf> electrodes were directly attached to a thin layer of Na metal for 30Â min, and a small amount of electrolyte was applied between them to provide a path for Na ion transfer. After the pre-sodiation process, the Gr/FeSO<ce:inf loc="post">4</ce:inf> electrode was detached and used to construct the 2032 half-cell. All experiments were performed in an Ar-filled glovebox with H<ce:inf loc="post">2</ce:inf> and O<ce:inf loc="post">2</ce:inf> levels of less than 0.1Â ppm because of the highly reactive nature of Na metal.</ce:para>
