The ultrathin Ti‐based nanosheets reduce the diffusion distance while rGO layers enhance conductivity. Incorporation of graphene into the titanate films produced efficient binder‐free anodes for ion storage. The resulting flexible NTO/rGO and KTO/rGO electrodes exhibited excellent rate performances and long cycling stability characterized by reversible capacities of 72 mA h g<sup>−1</sup> at 5 A g<sup>−1</sup> after 10000 cycles and 75 mA h g<sup>−1</sup> after 700 cycles at 2 A g<sup>−1</sup> for sodium and potassium ion batteries, respectively. These results demonstrate the superiority of the unique sandwich‐type electrodes.</p></abstract><abstract type="graphical" xml:lang="en"><p><b>Free‐standing integrated films</b> of ultrathin sodium titanate/potassium titanate nanosheets sandwiched between graphene layers were fabricated by confined transformation of assembled two‐dimensional MXene and reduced graphene oxide nanosheets. This hybridization produced films that can serve as the binder‐free anodes for sodium‐ion and potassium‐ion batteries with excellent durability and rate performance.<blockFixed xml:id="anie201803511-note-1001"><p>These authors contributed equally to this work.</p></note></noteGroup></header><body sectionsNumbered="no"><section xml:id="anie201803511-sec-0001"><p>Nowadays, the widespread application of flexible electronics has significantly boosted the development of flexible energy storage systems.<link href="#anie201803511-bib-0001"/> To build those flexible energy storage devices, two‐dimensional (2D) nanomaterials with outstanding mechanical flexibility and planar geometry have been considered as promising candidates.<link href="#anie201803511-bib-0002"/> Layered Ti‐based materials, especially sodium titanate (NTO) and potassium titanate (KTO), have been extensively studied as benchmark layered 2D electrode materials for sodium‐ion batteries (SIBs) and potassium‐ion batteries (PIBs) owing to low working voltage, suitable interlayer spacing for ions, high chemical stability, and environmental friendliness.<link href="#anie201803511-bib-0003"/> However, the sluggish ion insertion/extraction kinetics and substantial lattice expansion and strain for ion insertion/extraction, limit the electrochemical performance of titanate materials.<link href="#anie201803511-bib-0004"/> Constructing layered Ti‐based anodes with well‐confined layers, which is preferable for shortened diffusion distances, reducing large energy consumption of ion transport, and increasing specific surface area for exposed active sites have been proved to be an effective strategy to improve electrochemical kinetics.<link href="#anie201803511-bib-0004b"/>,<link href="#anie201803511-bib-0004c"/> Nevertheless the ultrathin layered Ti‐based nanosheets are preferable for good electrochemical performance, their synthesis remains a challenge because the few‐layered nanosheets have a tendency toward restacking and aggregating.<link href="#anie201803511-bib-0005"/></p><p>Integrating 2D materials into sandwiched structures prevents restacking of nanosheets as it was shown for phosphorene/graphene,<link href="#anie201803511-bib-0006"/> MnO<sub>2</sub>/graphene,<link href="#anie201803511-bib-0005b"/> antimonene/graphene,<link href="#anie201803511-bib-0007"/> and MoS<sub>2</sub>/graphene.<link href="#anie201803511-bib-0008"/> Nowadays, most of the reports on sandwiched structure follow this procedure: namely, 2D materials are synthesized or exfoliated in advance and then assembled into sandwiched structures.<link href="#anie201803511-bib-0001b #anie201803511-bib-0005b #anie201803511-bib-0006 #anie201803511-bib-0007 #anie201803511-bib-0008 #anie201803511-bib-0009"/> Although this method has been widely used, so far only a few materials such as MoS<sub>2</sub>, graphene, phosphorene, and MXene were employed to construct the sandwiched structures.<link href="#anie201803511-bib-0001b #anie201803511-bib-0006 #anie201803511-bib-0007 #anie201803511-bib-0008 #anie201803511-bib-0009b"/> The reason is that this method is limited to a range of the materials whose 2D structure can be synthesized or exfoliated and stabilized. For instance, fabrication of 2D ultrathin layered titanate is difficult. MXene, as a novel Ti‐based layered material, attracted much attention because of its good mechanical properties, abundant surface groups ‐T<sub><i>x</i></sub> (‐F, ‐O, ‐OH), and unique 2D planar structure.<link href="#anie201803511-bib-0001b #anie201803511-bib-0009b #anie201803511-bib-0010"/> Recently, several reports showed that it could be transformed to titanate. However, the aforementioned transformation of 2D MXene destroyed its ultrathin structure.<link href="#anie201803511-bib-0011"/></p><p>Herein, we firstly confined MXene with rGO nanosheets and then transformed MXene into ultrathin titanate with well‐confined layered structure. This process involving confinement and transformation of MXene produced flexible films composed of sandwiched titanate and rGO layers. This 2D arrangement of sandwiched structure was maintained after forceful transformation. The resulting sandwiched hybrid structure features good mechanical properties and combines advantages of ultrathin titanates and the excellent conductivity of ultrathin rGO nanosheets. Benefits of these unique sandwiched hybrids used to fabricate flexible titanate anodes for SIBs/PIBs include excellent rate performance and long cycling stability.</p><p>Figure <link href="#anie201803511-fig-0001"/> illustrates the procedure for preparation of flexible NTO/rGO films (experimental details in Supporting Information). First, MXene nanosheets (Supporting Information, Figures S1–S3) with negative surface charge and positively charged GO modified with polydiallyldimethylammonium (PDDA) with different mass ratios were employed to assembly hybrid MXene/GO films via vacuum filtration with assistance of electrostatic attraction (Supporting Information, Figure S4).<link href="#anie201803511-bib-0005b #anie201803511-bib-0009b #anie201803511-bib-0010b"/> After annealing at 450 °C in Ar atmosphere, the free‐standing MXene/rGO films with different ratio of rGO were achieved and denoted as MXene/rGO‐5 %, MXene/rGO‐10 % and MXene/rGO‐20 %, respectively (Supporting Information, Figure S5 and Experimental Section). Finally, the MXene/rGO films were transformed into flexible NTO/rGO films through hydrothermal process in the presence of oxidant H<sub>2</sub>O<sub>2</sub> and NaOH. The corresponding films are denoted as NTO/rGO‐5 %, NTO/rGO‐10 % and NTO/rGO‐20 %. Also, flexible KTO/rGO films were fabricated by using KOH.</p><figure xml:id="anie201803511-fig-0001"><title type="figureName">Figure</title><mediaResourceGroup><mediaResource alt="image" href="urn:x-wiley:14337851:media:anie201803511:anie201803511-fig-0001"/><mediaResource alt="magnified image" rendition="webHiRes" mimeType="image/png" href="graphic/anie201803511-fig-0001-m.png"/></mediaResourceGroup><caption><p>Illustration showing the preparation of flexible NTO/rGO films.</p></caption></figure><p>Owing to the similarity of these samples, NTO/rGO‐10 % was selected as the representative sample for detailed characterization. As shown in the digital photographs (Figure <link href="#anie201803511-fig-0002"/> a inset; Supporting Information, Figure S6), similar with GO film and MXene/GO‐10 % film (Supporting Information, Figures S7a, S8a,b), as‐prepared NTO/rGO‐10 % film showed excellent flexibility after the confined transformation process. Both side‐view scanning electron microscopy (SEM) and top‐view SEM images (Figure <link href="#anie201803511-fig-0002"/> a; Supporting Information, Figure S6b) are similar with those of rGO and MXene/GO‐10 % films (Supporting Information, Figures S7b, S8), demonstrating the sandwiched structure of NTO/rGO‐10 % film was achieved in confined transformation process. Additionally, the cross‐section transmission electron microscopy (TEM) image of the as‐prepared films clearly shows the sandwiched structure and the multilayer distribution of NTO and rGO (Figure <link href="#anie201803511-fig-0002"/> b). As demonstrated in high resolution TEM (HRTEM) images (Figure <link href="#anie201803511-fig-0002"/> c,d), both NTO and graphene layers show narrow thicknesses of 5–10 nm and they are close to each other. HRTEM images show that the average interlayer spacing of 0.84 nm, which coincides with the (001) facet of monoclinic Na<sub>2</sub>Ti<sub>3</sub>O<sub>7</sub>, demonstrating the confined MXene layer was transformed to the monoclinic Na<sub>2</sub>Ti<sub>3</sub>O<sub>7</sub> layer.<link href="#anie201803511-bib-0003b #anie201803511-bib-0004a"/></p><figure xml:id="anie201803511-fig-0002"><title type="figureName">Figure</title><mediaResourceGroup><mediaResource alt="image" href="urn:x-wiley:14337851:media:anie201803511:anie201803511-fig-0002"/><mediaResource alt="magnified image" rendition="webHiRes" mimeType="image/png" href="graphic/anie201803511-fig-0002-m.png"/></mediaResourceGroup><caption><p>SEM and TEM images of the NTO/rGO‐10 % hybrid film. a) Side‐view SEM image, digital photograph (inset). b) Cross‐sectional TEM image and c),d) HRTEM images (“N” refers to NTO, and “G” refers to rGO). e),f) TEM elemental mapping images of Na, Ti, O, and C elements.</p></caption></figure><p>Moreover, the sandwiched structure is further verified by energy‐dispersive X‐ray spectroscopy elemental mapping images (Figure <link href="#anie201803511-fig-0002"/> e,f) and lining scan (Supporting Information, Figure S9). It should be noted that the as‐prepared film shows similar element distribution and sandwiched structure with the MXene/GO‐10 % precursor (Supporting Information, Figure S10), demonstrating the NTO layers originate from the MXene sheets. This supports the idea that the confined transformation preserves the 2D planar nature and the sandwiched structure. A similar sandwiched structure can also be observed in the case of KTO/rGO‐10 % (Supporting Information, Figure S10). Thus, the sandwiched structures of layered titanate/rGO sheets can be fabricated via the aforementioned confined transformation process.</p><p>An X‐ray diffraction (XRD) pattern and X‐ray photoelectron spectroscopy (XPS) spectra were used to further investigate the structure and composition of NTO/rGO‐10 %. As can be seen from Figure <link href="#anie201803511-fig-0003"/> a, the phase coincides with that of reported Na<sub>2</sub>Ti<sub>3</sub>O<sub>7</sub> phase.<link href="#anie201803511-bib-0003b #anie201803511-bib-0004a"/> As compared with the XPS spectra of pristine MXene (Supporting Information, Figure S3c,d), the appearance of Na 1s, Ti‐O peaks in Ti 2p spectrum and the disappearance of the C‐Ti bond after the reaction, evidencing successful transformation of Ti<sub>3</sub>C<sub>2</sub>T<sub><i>x</i></sub> to NTO (Figure <link href="#anie201803511-fig-0003"/> b–d; Supporting Information, Figure S3).<link href="#anie201803511-bib-0003a #anie201803511-bib-0004a #anie201803511-bib-0011c"/> K 2p signals are also visible in the Supporting Information, Figure S12, confirming the similar transformation of Ti<sub>3</sub>C<sub>2</sub>T<sub><i>x</i></sub> MXene to KTO.<link href="#anie201803511-bib-0011c"/></p><figure xml:id="anie201803511-fig-0003"><title type="figureName">Figure</title><mediaResourceGroup><mediaResource alt="image" href="urn:x-wiley:14337851:media:anie201803511:anie201803511-fig-0003"/><mediaResource alt="magnified image" rendition="webHiRes" mimeType="image/png" href="graphic/anie201803511-fig-0003-m.png"/></mediaResourceGroup><caption><p>a) XRD pattern of NTO/rGO‐10 % film. b) XPS survey spectrum. c)–f) High‐resolution spectra: NTO/rGO‐10 % film; c) Ti 2p, d) Na 1s; the comparison of high‐resolution spectra of MXene/GO‐10 % and MXene/rGO‐10 %; e) Ti 2p, f) C 1s.</p></caption></figure><p>It is noteworthy that the unannealed MXene/GO film or pure MXene film were fragmented after transformation process to titanate. The XPS results reveal that the NTO/rGO‐10 % sandwiched film after confined transformation was preserved due to two reasons. First, as compared with the pristine Ti‐C (455.2 eV), Ti<sup>II</sup>O<sub><i>y</i></sub> (456.1 eV), Ti<sup>III</sup>O<sub><i>x</i></sub> (457.4 eV), and Ti‐O (458.9 eV) bonds in MXene/GO‐10 %, new TiO<sub><i>x</i></sub>C<sub><i>y</i></sub> (456.3 eV) bonds are formed in MXene/rGO‐10 % (Figure <link href="#anie201803511-fig-0003"/> e).<link href="#anie201803511-bib-0009b #anie201803511-bib-0012"/> The shift of C‐Ti (281.6 eV) and TiOC (Ti‐O‐C, 282.1 eV) bonds also verifies their transformation to TiO<sub><i>x</i></sub>C<sub><i>y</i></sub> (282.4 eV) bond (Figure <link href="#anie201803511-fig-0003"/> f). Both figures demonstrate that MXene is attached to carbon‐based sheet after annealing.<link href="#anie201803511-bib-0013"/> Second, as can be seen from the Supporting Information, Figures S13–S15, the absence or signal shrinkage related to the oxygen‐containing fuctional groups and the change of contact angle reveal that the annealing process removed most of hydrophilic functional groups, greatly decreasing hydrophilicity of MXene and rGO nanosheets, which prevented the subsequent rediffusion and consequently avoided the structural damage during transformation.<link href="#anie201803511-bib-0010a #anie201803511-bib-0014"/> Therefore, the key to preserve the integrated sandwiched structure of film after confined transformation process is in the simple annealing that forms new bonds between MXene and rGO and decreases hydrophilicity.</p><p>The electrochemical performance of the prepared NTO/rGO films was evaluated directly as a flexible electrode for sodium ion batteries (SIBs). Clearly, the NTO/rGO‐10 % electrode showed an initial discharge capacity of 1280 mA h g<sup>−1</sup> with a low initial Coulombic efficiency of about 26 %, which agrees with the cyclic voltammetry (CV) data (Supporting Information, Figure S16). The low Coulombic efficiency originates from irreversible reactions of Na<sup>+</sup> with functional groups and water on the surface of electrodes, and the formation of dense solid‐electrolyte interfacial (SEI) layer owing to the decomposition of the electrolyte.<link href="#anie201803511-bib-0004c #anie201803511-bib-0011a"/> The rate capabilities of NTO/rGO sandwiched structure were also evaluated at various current densities ranging from 0.1 to 5 A g<sup>−1</sup> (Figure <link href="#anie201803511-fig-0004"/> a). As compared to pure rGO and MXene, the NTO/rGO electrodes clearly exhibited an improved capacity, affirming the effectiveness of transformation for enhancing the specific capacity. Especially, the NTO/rGO‐10 % electrode afforded the sodiation capacities of 302, 226, 197, 174, 147, 127, and 97 mA h g<sup>−1</sup> at the current densities of 0.1, 0.2, 0.5, 1, 2, 3, and 5 A g<sup>−1</sup>, respectively.</p><figure xml:id="anie201803511-fig-0004"><title type="figureName">Figure</title><mediaResourceGroup><mediaResource alt="image" href="urn:x-wiley:14337851:media:anie201803511:anie201803511-fig-0004"/><mediaResource alt="magnified image" rendition="webHiRes" mimeType="image/png" href="graphic/anie201803511-fig-0004-m.png"/></mediaResourceGroup><caption><p>Electrochemical performance of the NTO/rGO films for SIBs and the KTO/rGO films for PIBs. a) Comparison of rate performance of NTO with different rGO content, rGO and MXene at various current densities. b) Comparison of rate performance of KTO/rGO‐10 %, rGO and MXene at various current densities. c) Long‐term cycling discharge capacities and Coulombic efficiencies of NTO/rGO‐10 % at 5 A g<sup>−1</sup>. d) Long‐term cycling discharge capacities and Coulombic efficiencies of KTO/rGO‐10 % at 2 A g<sup>−1</sup>.</p></caption></figure><p>To further investigate the effect of the ratio of NTO layers to rGO on the electrochemical performance, the rate performance of NTO with various rGO content are compared in the Supporting Information, Figure S17. As compared with NTO/rGO‐5 % whose the NTO layer‐to‐carbon layer thickness ratio is around three to four, NTO/rGO‐10 % with the NTO layer‐to‐carbon layer thickness ratio of around one is preferable for rapid electron and ion transfer.<link href="#anie201803511-bib-0009b"/> It is demonstrated by smaller polarization of NTO/rGO‐10 % at high current densities, which also confirms better electron and ion transport (Supporting Information, Figure S17a,b) and the smaller transport resistance (Supporting Information, Figure S17d).<link href="#anie201803511-bib-0009b"/> Additionally, owing to more active material, NTO, in the electrode, NTO/rGO‐10 % achieved higher reversible capacity than that of NTO/rGO‐20 %. Moreover, the NTO/rGO‐10 % sandwiched electrode showed good capacity retentions at various current densities, especially high rate 5 A g<sup>−1</sup> (Figure <link href="#anie201803511-fig-0004"/> c; Supporting Information, Figure S18). After 10 000 cycles, a specific capacity of 72 mA h g<sup>−1</sup> could be delivered. Furthermore, the technique of multi CV scans at various scan rates reveal that a high capacitive contribution ratio can be achieved under fast scan rate (Supporting Information, Figure S19). Consequently, NTO/rGO‐10 % achieved excellent rate capacity and long‐term cycling even at high current density. The excellent rate performance and long cycling stability are seldom reported in previous works on sodium titanate for SIBs (Supporting Information, Table S1).</p><p>The potassium storage properties of KTO/rGO‐10 % film as anode materials were also investigated. The KTO/rGO‐10 % electrode showed a high initial discharge capacity of 1094 mA h g<sup>−1</sup> with a low Coulombic efficiency about 24 %, agreeing with the CV result (Supporting Information, Figure S20). Moreover, KTO/rGO‐10 % afforded reversible capacities of 228, 162, 116 and 84 mA h g<sup>−1</sup> at the current densities of 0.1, 0.2, 0.5, 1 A g<sup>−1</sup>, respectively (Figure <link href="#anie201803511-fig-0004"/> b). Even at a high current density of 2 A g<sup>−1</sup>, it can still exhibit a high reversible capacity of 75 mA h g<sup>−1</sup> after 700 cycles (Figure <link href="#anie201803511-fig-0004"/> d). The electrochemical performance for PIBs is among the best of those of previously reported titanate and carbon‐based composite anodes (Supporting Information, Table S2).</p><p>The remarkable rate performance, long‐term cycle of the as‐prepared flexible NTO/rGO‐10 % for SIBs and KTO/rGO‐10 % for PIBs are observed because of the unique sandwiched structure achieved via confined transformation. Specifically, confined transformation method maintains the structural integrity and original sandwiched structure, which efficiently hinders self‐stacking of sheets. Besides, the coupled layers serve as confinement between each other. This avoids the use of binder, which has negative effect on batteries because it decreases energy density and might cause the side effects.<link href="#anie201803511-bib-0009b #anie201803511-bib-0015"/> Additionally, the well‐confined layers facilitate the ion travel through thin NTO or KTO layers in the unique sandwiched structure.<link href="#anie201803511-bib-0004b"/>,<link href="#anie201803511-bib-0004c"/> Importantly, graphene layers sandwiched between titanate layers not only serve as conductive pathways for electrons transport but also as buffer layers for lattice expansion and strain during continuous Na<sup>+</sup> or K<sup>+</sup> insertion and extraction during long cycles.<link href="#anie201803511-bib-0006"/> Furthermore, the sandwiched structure could improve structural stability and mechanical flexibility of the electrode materials.<link href="#anie201803511-bib-0006 #anie201803511-bib-0015 #anie201803511-bib-0016"/> Therefore, all of these features accelerate ions and electrons transport effectively, which is supported by the excellent rate performance and cycling stability of the as‐prepared electrodes for SIBs and PIBs.</p><p>In summary, we employed the confined transformation of MXene/rGO hybrid films to fabricate integrated films of titanate/rGO with sandwiched structure. Benefiting from the combination of the unique sandwiched structure, reduced diffusion distance originating from ultrathin NTO or KTO layers, enhanced electronic conductivity of graphene, and good compatibility between layered nanosheets, the flexible layer titanate/rGO films exhibited excellent performance and long cycling stability for SIBs and PIBs. Thus, the developed confined transformation method opens new opportunities to achieve a broad spectrum of hybrid 2D materials with sandwiched structures for sensing, photocatalysis and electrocatalysis.</p><section xml:id="anie201803511-sec-0002" type="acknowledgments"><title type="main">Acknowledgements</title><p>This work was financially supported by the National Key Research and Development Program of China (2016YFA0202604), the Natural Science Foundation of China (21606088, 51621001), the “Thousand Talents Program”, the Fundamental Research Funds for the Central Universities (2017ZD063), and the Australian Research Council (DE150101234, FL170100154).</p></section><section xml:id="anie201803511-sec-0003" type="conflictOfInterest"><title type="main">Conflict of interest</title><p>The authors declare no conflict of interest.</p></section></section><bibliography style="numbered"><bib xml:id="anie201803511-bib-0001"><citation type="other" xml:id="anie201803511-cit-0001"> </citation></bib><bib xml:id="anie201803511-bib-0001a"><label>1a</label><citation type="journal" xml:id="anie201803511-cit-0002"><author><givenNames>M.
